Section B. Assessment Framework

(For Reference)

▼

Five Levels of AI Use in Teaching Contexts

Level 1: Awareness & Exploration

Understanding AI capabilities and limitations, academic integrity, and copyright. Classroom demonstrations include sentence rewriting, summarizing, and keyword extraction.

Level 2: Assistance & Efficiency

Teachers use AI for lesson preparation, drafting questions and comments, and data organization. Students use AI for language practice, summarizing key points, paraphrasing, and preparing speaking scripts. All AI-assisted work must retain human review and clearly mark AI participation.

Level 3: Augmentation & Co-creation

View AI as a peer partner for multi-version brainstorming, perspective comparison, and argument strength analysis. Students must submit documentation of their questioning process, selection rationale, and human revision.

Level 4: Automation & Embedded Assessment

Establish structured teaching workflows with pre-class guiding questions and vocabulary, in-class formative checks, and post-class individualized practice. Implement semi-automated marking aligned with rubrics, followed by teacher review.

Level 5: Data-driven Optimization & Innovation

Use class and course-level data (participation rates, error patterns, and progress metrics) to optimize materials and teaching methods. Develop new task types such as scenario simulations, self-built AI tutors, and cross-disciplinary projects.
